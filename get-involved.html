Ways to Get Involved to Help Fight Sex Trafficking

News and updates about the fight to end sexual exploitation and trafficking

What is Sex Trafficking?

  1. Learn more about the signs of human trafficking. If you notice any of the following suspicious signs, contact the law enforcement or 1-888-373-7888 National Human Trafficking Resource Center to report the situation.
    • Signs of physical abuse
    • Unable to speak to people without someone’s supervision
    • Poor living conditions
    • Fearful and submissive
    • Underage and involved in prostitution
    • Unpaid or severely underpaid
  2. Volunteer for non-profits for against human trafficking in your community. See referral directory https://humantraffickinghotline.org/training-resources/referral-directory
  3. Organize a community event to raise awareness or arrange a fundraiser event and donate to organizations that help human trafficking victims.
  4. Ask the school district your community belongs to to organize events to raise awareness, especially since sex traffickers usually target underage people. As a student or faculty of a higher educational institution, request human trafficking related subject be including in university curricula. Take action on campus by organize fund raising or establish a club that help spread awareness.
  5. Visit business who help support sex trafficking survivors, or as a business owner, getting involved by hiring more employees who survived sex trafficking.
  6. As professionals who are involved in health care, legal, or journal fields, help by using your professional knowledge to help the survivors or organizations that help fight human trafficking.
  7. If you would like to help the survivors and have any special skills, such as cooking, writing, extensive knowledge and math and science, join our mailing list to sign up to be a volunteer to teach our survivors the skills you have!
  8. The Genesis Project Seattle provides a way to Donate to the cause
